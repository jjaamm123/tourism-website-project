Kathmandu Valley Heritage Tour

Explore UNESCO World Heritage Sites, ancient temples, and royal palaces

Tour Overview

Experience the rich cultural heritage of Kathmandu Valley through this immersive 3-day tour. Visit three UNESCO World Heritage Sites: Kathmandu Durbar Square, Patan Durbar Square, and Bhaktapur Durbar Square. Discover intricate wood carvings, ancient temples, and traditional Newari architecture.

Our expert local guides will share fascinating stories about the Malla kings, Hindu and Buddhist traditions, and the living heritage that makes Kathmandu Valley unique.

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Kathmandu Heritage

Start your journey at Kathmandu Durbar Square, the historical seat of the Malla kings. Visit the Kumari Ghar (Living Goddess Temple), Hanuman Dhoka, and the Taleju Temple. In the afternoon, climb to Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple) for panoramic views of the valley.

Day 2: Patan's Artistic Heritage

Explore Patan Durbar Square, known for its exquisite metalwork and stone carvings. Visit the Patan Museum, Golden Temple, and Krishna Mandir. Watch local artisans create traditional metal statues and thangka paintings.

Day 3: Bhaktapur's Medieval Charm

Travel to Bhaktapur, the best-preserved medieval city in Nepal. Wander through cobblestone streets, visit pottery square, and admire the 55-Window Palace. Enjoy authentic Newari cuisine before returning to Kathmandu.
